Our teachers are trained in the D.I.S.C.O.V.E.R.Y. World-class Infrastructure

More than a classroom
Learning with 20 experiential zones

Welcome to 10,000 sq. ft. of limitless learning. Our R.E.D™ infrastructure - Reflect, Express, Discover - draws from the Reggio Emilia approach, where the environment becomes the teacher. With 20 immersive experience zones, your child is free to explore and learn through play, indoors and outdoors. Every space is designed to inspire reflection, expression, and discovery. Here, education flows with curiosity, allowing kids to learn wherever their imagination takes them.

Learning comes alive at RISE Preschool

Play meets education at our experiential zones. Here, hands-on activities encourage exploration, learning and creativity through experience. Let the adventure begin!

1. Library

A quiet space that encourages a love for reading and storytelling, helping children expand their imagination and language skills.
Key Focus: Language development, literacy, and imaginative thinking.
Activities: Storytime sessions, book browsing, and early reading exercises.

2. Discovery Zone

This area is designed to challenge cognitive skills through puzzles, problem-solving, and interactive brain games.
Key Focus: Critical thinking, memory, and problem-solving.
Activities: Puzzles, memory games, and building activities.

3. Pretend and Play Zone

A role-playing space where children engage in imaginative scenarios, building social and communication skills.
Key Focus: Social interaction, empathy, and imaginative play.
Activities: Pretend kitchens and shops, dressing up to act out real-world roles.

4. Discovery Path / Traffic Path

A marked path that simulates real-world traffic situations, teaching road safety and encouraging physical activity.
Key Focus: Physical coordination, safety awareness, and problem-solving.
Activities: Navigating traffic signs, riding tricycles, and pedestrian awareness.

5. Ball Pit

A fun area where children can dive into a pool of soft balls, enhancing physical coordination and motor skills.
Key Focus: Physical activity, sensory play, and gross motor development.
Activities: Jumping, diving, and balance exercises.

6. Wall Climbing Zone

A structured climbing wall to help develop strength, coordination, and problem-solving as children navigate their way up.
Key Focus: Physical strength, motor skills, and confidence-building.
Activities: Basic climbing challenges with various difficulty levels.

7. STEM Zone

A hands-on learning area where children explore science, technology, engineering and math concepts through playful activities.
Key Focus: Scientific thinking, problem-solving and innovation.
Activities: Building simple machines, exploring magnets and conducting science experiments.

8. Math and Manipulatives Lab

A dedicated space for hands-on activities that reinforce mathematical concepts such as counting, patterns, and spatial awareness.
Key Focus: Numeracy skills, logical reasoning, and critical thinking.
Activities: Shape sorting, block building, and counting games.

9. Brain Gym

An activity zone that promotes cognitive growth and mental agility through brain-stimulating games and exercises.
Key Focus: Memory, concentration, and cognitive development.
Activities: Matching games, memory exercises, and brain teasers.

10. Emoji Wall

An interactive wall where children use emojis to identify and express their emotions, helping them develop emotional intelligence.
Key Focus: Emotional awareness, self-expression, and communication.
Activities: Identifying emotions, role-playing scenarios, and group discussions on feelings.

11. Gardening Zone

A dedicated zone to engage children in nature and hands-on learning about plants and growth cycles.
Key Focus: Environmental awareness, responsibility, and sensory exploration.
Activities: Planting seeds, watering plants, harvesting vegetables, exploring bugs and insects, and composting.

12. Language and Vocabulary Zone

A zone dedicated to improving language skills through storytelling, vocabulary games, and conversational exercises.
Key Focus: Language development, communication, and literacy.
Activities: Vocabulary cards, storytelling, and word association games.

14. Art Studio

A vibrant space where children can express their creativity through drawing, painting and crafting.
Key Focus: Fine motor skills, creativity and self-expression.
Activities: Painting, drawing, crafting and exploring different art mediums.

14. Performing Arts Studio

An interactive zone for children to express themselves through drama, dance, and role-play.
Key Focus: Confidence, communication, and emotional expression.
Activities: Role-play scenarios, dance routines, and mini performances.

15. Water Play Zone

A fun, safe area where children engage in water-based activities to learn about buoyancy, volume, and sensory stimulation.
Key Focus: Sensory development, coordination, and basic scientific principles.
Activities: Pouring, floating experiments, and water toys.

16. Sand Play Zone

Encouraging tactile exploration and imaginative play, this zone allows children to manipulate sand while enhancing their motor skills.
Key Focus: Sensory awareness, fine motor development, and creative exploration.
Activities: Building sandcastles, creating shapes, and digging.

17. Sensory Walk

A stimulating pathway with different textures and surfaces for children to explore, helping them develop tactile and proprioceptive skills.
Key Focus: Sensory exploration and motor development.
Activities: Walking barefoot on various surfaces and balancing exercises.

18. Music Sensory Wall

A wall fitted with musical instruments and interactive components for children to experiment with sounds and rhythms.
Key Focus: Musical exploration, auditory development, and creativity.
Activities: Drumming, tapping, and exploring different tones and rhythms.

19. Nature and Outdoor Play Zone

A space where children engage with the natural world, learning about plants, animals, and environmental sustainability.
Key Focus: Environmental awareness, responsibility, and sensory exploration.
Activities: Nature walks, observing plants and animals, and participating in eco-friendly activities.

20. Pottery Zone

A creative space where children work with clay, shaping and molding objects, helping them develop fine motor skills and patience.
Key Focus: Artistic expression, fine motor control, and creativity.
Activities: Clay modeling, creating simple pottery, and working with hand tools.

GROWTH MINDSET

Our belief that 'Every Child Can' is inspired by Carol Dweck's growth mindset - the idea that abilities grow with effort and support. Partnering with GEM™, we've woven this mindset into our curriculum from day one. We teach kids to embrace challenges, learn from mistakes and persevere. In our nurturing environment, every child is empowered to explore, experiment, and discover their unique strengths. By celebrating effort and resilience over perfection, we instil a lifelong love for learning.Our teachers play a key role, offering consistent challenges and positive reinforcement to build confidence, curiosity, and optimism.

TODDLER PROGRAMME (AGES 1.5 YEARS+)

FIRST STEPS INTO THE WORLD OF WONDER.

Our Toddler programme provides a nurturing and safe environment where young children take their first steps toward discovery. With an emphasis on sensory exploration and emotional security, we focus on:
Developing motor skills and coordination.
Building early social interactions and communication through songs, play and movement.
Creating a strong foundation for curiosity and independent exploration.

PLAYGROUP (AGES 2 TO 3 YEARS)

DISCOVERING THROUGH SENSORY PLAY.

In Playgroup, children continue their discovery journey by engaging in sensory-rich activities designed to foster curiosity and self-expression. At this stage, children are introduced to: Social skills development through play-based interaction. Sensory exploration using textures, colours and sounds. Early language skills through storytelling, rhymes and songs.

NURSERY (AGES 3 TO 4 YEARS)

WHERE CURIOSITY TAKES ROOT.

Nursery is where a child's natural curiosity blossoms. With hands-on, play-based learning experiences, we encourage children to dive deeper into the world around them. Here, the focus is on: Early literacy and numeracy skills through hands-on engaging activities. Problem-solving and critical thinking via creative exploration. Strengthening social skills through cooperative play and teamwork.

JUNIOR KINDERGARTEN (AGES 4 TO 5 YEARS)

EXPLORING NEW POSSIBILITIES

In Jr. KG, children begin to apply the skills they have developed in more structured ways. We aim to ignite their imagination and expand their academic foundations. Key areas include: Introduction to early reading and writing. Basic math concept.

SENIOR KINDERGARTEN (AGES 5 TO 6 YEARS)

RISING AS CONFIDENT LEARNERS.

As children approach formal schooling, Sr. KG prepares them for academic success while fostering empathy, leadership and independence. We focus on: Strengthening reading, writing and problem-solving skills. Encouraging leadership and collaboration through group projects. Preparing children for the next stage of their academic journey by emphasising critical thinking and social-emotional development.
